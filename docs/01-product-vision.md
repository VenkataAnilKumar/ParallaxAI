# Parallax — Product Vision

## The One-Line Pitch

> One question. Ten specialized agents. One complete picture.

---

## The Problem

Research today is broken in three ways:

### 1. It's slow
A consultant doing competitive research spends 2-3 days manually searching, reading, and synthesizing. A VC doing due diligence on a startup spends a full week gathering market data, competitor intel, team background, and financial context. A founder validating a market idea spends days reading reports, forums, and news — and still isn't confident in the answer.

### 2. It's shallow
A single person (or a single AI) can only look at one thing at a time. They search sequentially. They miss angles. They stop when they feel they have "enough." The result is research that covers the obvious and misses the important.

### 3. It's unverified
One source says the market is $5B. Another says $2B. One article says the regulation changed last year. Another references the old law. Nobody cross-checks. Research produced by one agent — or one person — has no internal verification mechanism.

---

## The Solution

**Parallax is a multi-agent research network.**

When you ask Parallax a question, it doesn't send one agent to find the answer. It deploys a coordinated team of specialized research agents simultaneously — each investigating a different angle of your question in parallel. A cross-validation agent then checks for contradictions and scores confidence. A synthesis agent assembles everything into one structured, sourced, and scored research report.

```
You ask: "Should I build a B2B payments product for SMBs in Southeast Asia?"

Parallax deploys:
├── Market Intelligence Agent    → market size, growth, trends
├── Competitor Agent             → who's already in the market
├── Regulatory Agent             → payment regulations per country
├── Consumer Sentiment Agent     → what SMBs actually say about payments
├── Financial Agent              → revenue models, unit economics benchmarks
├── News Intelligence Agent      → recent events, funding, M&A activity
├── Academic Agent               → research papers on fintech adoption
├── Risk Agent                   → political, economic, execution risks
├── Cross-Validator Agent        → checks all agents for contradictions
└── Synthesis Agent              → assembles final report

Delivered in 5-8 minutes.
What used to take 3 days.
```

## Why Now

Three things converged to make Parallax possible in 2026:

**1. LLM APIs are good enough**
Claude Opus 4.6 and GPT-4o can reason deeply, synthesize across sources, and identify contradictions reliably. A year ago they couldn't.

**2. Web search APIs exist**
Tavily, Serper, and Exa provide real-time web search that agents can call programmatically. This wasn't reliable 18 months ago.

**3. Parallel agent execution is proven**
The infrastructure to run 10 agents simultaneously, collect results, and synthesize is now straightforward to build. The engineering is hard but solved.

## What Makes Parallax Unique

### Cross-Validation — Nobody Else Does This
Every other research tool (Perplexity, ChatGPT, even other agent tools) gives you one answer from one perspective. Parallax runs a dedicated cross-validation agent that:
- Compares findings across all research agents
- Flags contradictions explicitly
- Assigns confidence scores to every claim
- Identifies gaps where no agent found reliable information

You don't just get research. You get research you can trust.

### Parallel Depth — Not Sequential Breadth
Other tools search sequentially. Parallax researches all angles simultaneously. This means the financial data, the regulatory context, the competitor landscape, and the consumer sentiment are all researched to the same depth — at the same time.

### Structured Output — Actionable, Not Just Informational
Parallax doesn't return a wall of text. Every report is structured:
- Executive summary (3 bullets)
- Findings by angle (with sources)
- Contradictions found (with resolution)
- Confidence scores per section
- Gaps identified (what we couldn't find)
- Recommended next questions
