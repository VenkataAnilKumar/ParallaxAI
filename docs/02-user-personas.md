## Persona 1 — The VC Partner

**Name:** Arjun Mehta
**Role:** Partner at early-stage VC fund
**Location:** Singapore / Remote
**Team size:** 4 partners, 2 analysts

### Background
Arjun sees 200+ pitches per month. His fund focuses on B2B SaaS and fintech in Southeast Asia. He makes 8-10 investments per year. Each investment requires deep due diligence — market size, competitive landscape, founder background, regulatory risks, financial benchmarks.

### Current Research Process
```
Day 1: Read pitch deck, Google company + founder
Day 2: Analyst does market research (3-4 hours)
Day 3: Call with industry expert ($500/hour)
Day 4: Internal discussion, more research gaps filled
Day 5: Decision
```
Total research time: 8-12 hours per deal, across 5 days.

### Pain Points
- Too many deals, not enough time for deep research
- Analysts spend 70% of time on research, 30% on analysis
- Misses deals because research takes too long
- Contradictory information from different sources — no way to resolve
- Research quality varies by analyst

### How Arjun Uses Parallax
```
Receives pitch deck at 9am
Asks Parallax: "Due diligence on [startup name] —
                market, competition, regulatory, team background"
Gets report by 9:15am
Makes preliminary decision before noon call with founder
```

### What He Pays
- $500/month (Pro plan)
- Justified by: one extra deal per quarter = fund-level ROI

## Persona 2 — The Strategy Consultant

**Name:** Sarah Chen
**Role:** Senior Manager, Strategy Consulting
**Location:** London
**Team size:** 4-person project team

### Background
Sarah works at a top-tier strategy firm. Her projects are 8-12 weeks. The first 2-3 weeks are always research-heavy — understanding the client's industry, mapping competitors, sizing the market. This is where junior staff spend most of their time.

### Current Research Process
```
Week 1-2: Junior consultants research manually
          Desk research + secondary sources
          Each analyst owns one workstream
          Weekly sync to combine findings
          Often find contradictions too late
```

### Pain Points
- Research takes 30-40% of total project time
- Junior staff research quality is inconsistent
- Multiple analysts find contradictory data — reconciliation is painful
- Can't show research process to clients (messy, undocumented)
- Last-minute research gaps discovered during client presentations

### How Sarah Uses Parallax
```
Project kickoff: Run Parallax on industry + client's market
Get structured research in 10 minutes
Junior staff spend time on analysis, not gathering
Show clients the sourced, confidence-scored research
```

### What She Pays
- $200/month personal (expenses reimbursed by firm)
- Recommends firm license: $2,000/month for team of 20

## Persona 3 — The Startup Founder

**Name:** Priya Rajan
**Role:** Founder, pre-seed B2B startup
**Location:** Bangalore
**Team size:** 2 (co-founder + herself)

### Background
Priya is building a legal tech product for Indian law firms. She has limited time and no budget for research firms. She needs to understand the market, validate her assumptions, find competitors she hasn't heard of, and prep for investor conversations — all while building the product.

### Current Research Process
```
Evenings + weekends: Manual Googling
Reddit/LinkedIn for qualitative insights
Crunchbase for competitor funding
Industry reports (expensive, often outdated)
Asking people in her network
Total: 3-4 hours per research question, inconsistent quality
```

### Pain Points
- No time — building product during the day
- No money for research tools or consultants
- Worried about competitors she doesn't know about
- Investor questions she can't answer confidently
- Market size numbers she can't verify

### How Priya Uses Parallax
```
Asks Parallax: "What is the legal tech market in India —
                size, players, regulations, pain points"
Gets comprehensive report
Uses it directly in investor deck (with citations)
Runs it again when she finds a new competitor
```

### What She Pays
- $49/month (Starter plan)
- Justified by: replaces hours of manual research per week

## Persona 4 — The Corporate Strategy Analyst

**Name:** Marcus Williams
**Role:** Strategy Analyst, Fortune 500 Tech Company
**Location:** Austin, Texas
**Team size:** 8-person strategy team

### Background
Marcus's team advises the C-suite on competitive positioning, market entry, and M&A targets. They produce weekly competitive intelligence briefings and quarterly market landscape reports. Research is continuous, not project-based.

### Current Research Process
```
Weekly: Monitor 20+ competitor websites, news sources
        Summarize manually for leadership briefings
        Use Gartner/Forrester reports ($$$)
        Internal analyst time: 15 hours/week on research alone
```

### Pain Points
- Research is reactive, not proactive
- Miss competitor moves until they're public
- Reports take too long — by the time they're done, info is stale
- Hard to justify research headcount to leadership
- Contradictory analyst reports with no way to reconcile

### How Marcus Uses Parallax
```
Sets up standing research: "Monitor these 12 competitors weekly"
Gets briefing every Monday morning automatically
Runs deep research when a competitor announces something
Shares sourced, confidence-scored reports with C-suite
```

### What He Pays
- Team plan: $500/month
- Replaces: 1 junior analyst role ($80k/year)

## Persona 5 — The Investigative Journalist

**Name:** Aiko Tanaka
**Role:** Senior Reporter, Tech Publication
**Location:** Tokyo / Remote
**Beat:** AI, startups, enterprise tech

### Background
Aiko writes 3-4 in-depth pieces per month. Each requires significant background research — company history, financial context, competitive landscape, expert opinions, regulatory environment. She files under deadline pressure.

### Current Research Process
```
2-3 hours per story: Background research
Google, LinkedIn, Crunchbase, court records
Read 20+ articles for context
Call 2-3 sources to verify facts
Still worried about missing something important
```

### Pain Points
- Research time competes with writing time
- Hard to cross-verify claims from different sources
- Deadline pressure means research is often shallow
- Missing context that would make the story better
- Can't show research trail to editors easily

### How Aiko Uses Parallax
```
Starts each story: Run Parallax on the subject
Gets background in 8 minutes instead of 3 hours
Cross-validated claims she can trust
Gaps identified — knows exactly what she needs to verify personally
More time for interviews and writing
```

### What She Pays
- $49/month (Starter)
- Publication expense account
